# Bad News Emails

## The Core Principle

Bad news is never bad news. It is a "learning opportunity," a "pivot," or a "chance to realign."

Your job is to deliver failures in a way that sounds like strategy.

---

## The Reframe Taxonomy

Before writing a bad news email, translate the situation:

| What Actually Happened | What You Write |
|---|---|
| We missed the deadline | "We've decided to prioritize quality over speed" |
| The project was cancelled | "We're sunsetting this initiative to focus on higher-impact work" |
| We shipped a bug | "We've identified an area for improvement in our release process" |
| Nobody used the feature | "We're gathering data to better understand adoption patterns" |
| You lost the client | "We've decided to evolve our portfolio strategy" |
| The system went down | "We experienced an unexpected availability event" |
| You made a mistake | "There was a miscommunication around expectations" |
| Nobody knows why it broke | "We're investigating the root cause with full transparency" |

## Template: The Missed Deadline

```
Subject: Update — [Project Name] Timeline

Hi team,

I wanted to share a quick update on [Project Name] and provide some
additional context around our current timeline.

After careful consideration and a thorough review of our priorities,
we've made the decision to extend our target date to [new vague date].
This gives us the opportunity to ensure we're delivering something
that truly meets the bar we've set for ourselves.

The quality of what we're building is paramount, and we believe
this additional time will allow us to address a few open items
that are critical to the long-term success of this initiative.

I'll share a more detailed update at our next sync. In the meantime,
please don't hesitate to reach out with any questions.

Thanks for your continued support and partnership.

Best,
[You]
```

**Note**: A new date was not given. "Next sync" is not scheduled.

## Template: The Cancelled Project

```
Subject: Strategic Update — [Project Name]

Hi team,

I'm writing to share an important update regarding [Project Name].

After a thorough evaluation and alignment with leadership, we've made
the decision to sunset this initiative. This was not a decision made
lightly — the work you've all contributed has been exceptional and
has provided invaluable learnings that will inform our direction
going forward.

As we continue to sharpen our focus on our highest-priority
investments, we believe this change positions us to deliver
greater impact in areas where we can win.

More details on next steps to come. Please feel free to reach out
directly if you have questions.

Thank you for everything you've brought to this work.

Best,
[You]
```

**Translation**: It didn't work and leadership lost interest. The "learnings" will never be referenced again.

## Template: The Incident (System Outage / Bug in Prod)

```
Subject: [RESOLVED] Availability Event — [System Name]

Hi all,

I wanted to provide a brief summary of the availability event
we experienced earlier today affecting [System Name].

**What happened**: At approximately [time], we observed elevated
error rates in [vague component]. Our on-call team was immediately
engaged and worked quickly to restore service.

**Current status**: The issue has been resolved as of [time].
All systems are operating normally.

**Next steps**: We are conducting a full post-mortem to understand
the root cause and identify opportunities to strengthen our
reliability posture going forward. We will share findings with
the relevant stakeholders.

We apologize for any disruption this may have caused and appreciate
your patience.

Best,
[You]
```

**Note**: "Elevated error rates" means it was completely down. "Relevant stakeholders" will never see the post-mortem. The post-mortem may not happen.

## The Blame Distribution Strategy

When something went wrong and ownership is contested, use this structure:

```
"This was the result of a combination of factors across multiple
workstreams, and we believe a cross-functional retrospective
will help us understand the full picture."
```

**Effect**: Everyone is responsible. No one is responsible.

---

## The Silver Lining Closer

Every bad news email must end with fake optimism.

Choose one:

```
"We're excited about what this means for [vague future]."
"This positions us well for the next phase of [thing]."
"We see this as an opportunity to build something even better."
"The team has shown incredible resilience throughout this process."
"We're proud of the progress we've made and energized by what's ahead."
```

These sentences mean nothing. They are required.
